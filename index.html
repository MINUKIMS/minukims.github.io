Welcome to my page! I am a Master’s student in the SSSC Lab at KAIST (Statistical Speech and Sound Computing Laboratory), advised by Prof. Hoirin Kim. My academic interests lie at the intersection of linguistics and artificial intelligence. In particular, I enjoy exploring phonetics, phonology, and language typology, and I am passionate about connecting linguistic theories with computational approaches for low-resource language processing. I am also interested in multilingual automatic speech recognition (ASR) and more broadly in advancing research on the linguistics–AI intersection to bridge human language theory and machine learning methods.
